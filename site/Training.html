Dog training

Until they're 16 weeks old, a puppy cannot properly control their bladder, and therefore can’t always wait for someone to take them outside. Training pads are a great way to keep your floors clean as well as preventing a puppy from getting punished for something they can’t control. For very young puppies this is very important, as before their vaccinations taking them outside to use the bathroom can expose them to deadly viruses. Here is a guide on how to train your puppy as they grow up:

\

When does your puppy have to pee?

Puppies like to use the restroom right after waking up from a nap, after eating, and after playing. So if any of these occur, take your puppy to their pad immediately and wait till they go, rewarding them with praise if they do. Also it’s a good idea to place the pad near (but not right next to) where they sleep so they have somewhere to go after they wake up.

What if your puppy doesn't use the pad?

Make sure your puppy stays near you while your home, so if you catch them peeing somewhere else, interrupt and immediately take them to the pad. Use some consistent phrase telling them to go to the bathroom, such as “Go tinkle!” or “Use the potty!” So long as you're consistent, they’ll be trained in no time!

Would you like to transition your dog to outdoor training?

As your puppy gets better bladder control, move the pad closer to the door each day, eventually taking it outside. Once your puppy is going on the pad outside, they will transition quickly to going outside even when the pad is removed.

Congratulations on training your pet!
